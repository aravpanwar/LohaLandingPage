Problem Overview

Dual-use precursor chemicals are essential in industries like pharmaceuticals, agriculture, and manufacturing, but they are also misused in the production of illegal drugs. For instance, Acetic Anhydride is used to convert opium into heroin, while Ephedrine is used in the production of methamphetamine.

The Government of India enforces strict regulations on seven widely misused precursor chemicals under the Regulation of Controlled Substances Order, 2013. Firms handling these chemicals must report activities like manufacturing, distribution, and export to the Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB) and obtain a Unique Registration Number (URN). Any violation of these regulations is punishable under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985.

The Challenge

Despite regulations, some firms exploit the system by creating false documentation, diverting chemicals from legal to illegal channels. These firms fabricate records of the movement of precursor chemicals to legitimate firms while trafficking them for the illicit manufacture of narcotic drugs or psychotropic substances.

Expected Solution

We aim to develop a hardware/software solution that enables end-to-end tracking of the seven domestically controlled precursor chemicals. This system would track the journey of these chemicals from manufacture to their final destination or export, ensuring transparency, real-time monitoring, and compliance with regulatory requirements. By leveraging blockchain technology for immutable records and IoT devices for real-time tracking, this solution will prevent the illegal diversion of these chemicals.
